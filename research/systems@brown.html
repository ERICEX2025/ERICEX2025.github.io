Systems@Brown

May 2023 – Aug 2023 Undergraduate Research Assistant

Designed cryptographic protocols to preserve user privacy and accountability in the $245B online advertising market. As an undergraduate research assistant, I worked on implementing and validating privacy-preserving advertising systems using advanced cryptographic techniques.

Validation Prototype

Implemented a Python-based validation prototype simulating interactions between advertisers, publishers, platforms, and users. The system ensured user privacy using anonymous credentials, zero-knowledge proofs, and blind signatures, providing a foundation for privacy-preserving advertising systems.

Protocol Implementation

Reproduced and extended protocols from our group's privacy-preserving ads paper. Leveraged the anonymous-credentials-lite library to implement credential issuance and verification, as well as blind-signature flows across users, platforms, and publishers.

Learning & Development

Ramped from no prior cryptography background to a working Python prototype with zero-knowledge proofs. This involved deep learning of cryptographic concepts including anonymous credentials, zero-knowledge proofs, and blind signatures, demonstrating the ability to quickly master complex technical domains.

Performance Evaluation

Ran microbenchmarks to evaluate latency and overhead for each party under realistic workloads and adversaries. This performance analysis helped validate the practical feasibility of the cryptographic protocols and informed design decisions for the privacy-preserving advertising system.

Reflection

Working at Systems@Brown was a transformative experience that introduced me to the world of cryptographic research. Starting with no prior background in cryptography, I learned to implement complex protocols involving zero-knowledge proofs, anonymous credentials, and blind signatures. The opportunity to work with Professors Malte Schwarzkopf and Anna Lysyanskaya gave me invaluable insights into how research is conducted at the intersection of systems and cryptography. This experience deepened my appreciation for the importance of privacy in modern systems and the sophisticated techniques required to achieve it.

Advised by Professor Malte Schwarzkopf and Professor Anna Lysyanskaya
