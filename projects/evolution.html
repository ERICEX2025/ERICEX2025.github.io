Evolution

Nov 2021 – Dec 2021 Brown CS15

As part of Brown University's CS15 course, I built Evolution: a CS15 Arcade featuring multiple Flappy Bird variations powered by neuroevolution. The project combined neural networks, evolutionary algorithms, and game development to create an AI that learns and improves through natural selection. I implemented Manual Flappy Bird, Multiplayer Flappy Bird, and Smart Flappy Bird (with a population of up to 50 AI agents that evolve over generations), along with other arcade games like Tetris and Doodle Jump, all integrated into a unified modular framework.

Manual & Multiplayer Flappy Bird

Implemented a fully functional Flappy Bird game with gravity simulation, pipe generation with semi-random gaps, collision detection, and score tracking. The bird falls under gravity and jumps with spacebar, with pipes scrolling at constant rates. I also created a multiplayer variant with two birds (one controlled by spacebar, one by up arrow) that compete simultaneously. Both variants include game over conditions, high score tracking, and pause/restart functionality.

Smart Flappy Bird & Neuroevolution

Developed Smart Flappy Bird with a population of up to 50 AI agents that evolve over generations. Each bird has its own neural network with input nodes (normalized environment data), hidden layers, and output nodes for decision-making. I implemented forward propagation using matrix multiplication and sigmoid activation functions. The evolutionary algorithm uses selection (choosing elite birds based on fitness), mutation (random weight changes), and generational progression. The system tracks fitness (different from score), displays generation statistics, and automatically restarts with evolved populations. Over time, the birds learn to navigate pipes successfully.

CS15 Arcade Framework

Designed and implemented a modular arcade framework with a home screen that allows users to select between Manual Flappy Bird, Multiplayer Flappy Bird, Smart Flappy Bird, and other arcade games (Tetris, Doodle Jump). The framework enforces consistent functionality across all games: quit, return to home, pause/unpause, and restart capabilities. I used polymorphism and delegation to create a unified architecture where each game implements a common interface while maintaining its unique logic. The design emphasizes extensibility, making it easy to add new games without modifying existing code.

Reflection

Evolution was one of the most challenging and rewarding projects in CS15, serving as my introduction to machine learning and neuroevolution. Implementing neural networks from scratch taught me the fundamentals of forward propagation, matrix multiplication, and activation functions. Building the evolutionary algorithm with selection and mutation gave me hands-on experience with genetic algorithms and fitness-based optimization. Watching a population of 50 birds evolve from completely random behavior to successfully navigating pipes over generations was both fascinating and addictive—I spent hours bird-watching!

The project also reinforced critical software design principles. Designing the arcade framework with polymorphism and proper delegation taught me the importance of extensibility and modularity. Creating a unified interface for multiple games while maintaining their unique behaviors required careful architectural planning. The experience debugging neuroevolution algorithms—where small changes in selection rates, mutation rates, or fitness functions could dramatically affect learning—gave me appreciation for the iterative nature of machine learning development. This project demonstrated how elegant design and algorithmic thinking come together to create something truly impressive.
